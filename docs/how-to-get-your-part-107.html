How to Get Your Part 107

The Part 107 exam is required for those who intend to fly a drone for commercial purposes.

Requirements to Take the Exam

  1. Be at least 16 years old
  2. Be able to read, write, speak and understand English
  3. Be in a physical and mental condition to safely fly a drone
  4. Pass the aeronautical exam: “Unmanned Aircraft General - Small (UAG)”

Process to Obtain Your License

Step 1: Obtain an FAA tracking number through IACRA by creating a profile

Step 2: Schedule your exam at a FAA-approved testing center. You will need your tracking number. As of 2021, the examination fee is $175.

Step 3: Pass the exam! The two hour exam will be on a computer. You are allowed to bookmark any questions you are unsure of. A booklet with aeronautical charts is provided.

Step 4: Complete FAA Form 8710-13 (Remote Pilot Certificate and/or Rating Application) using IACRA.

Remember to do the online recruitment training every 24 months to maintain your license

Studying For Part 107

A great starter video would be from Tony and Chelsea Northup. Tony provides a brief overview of the main topics that will be on the exam.

The official FAA study guide can be found here: https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation/media/remote_pilot_study_guide.pdf

A breakdown of key parts to study

  • Chapter 2: all airspace (controlled, uncontrolled, special, other), NOTAMs

  • Chapter 3a: learn how to read METARS

  • Chapter 3b: density altitude, what impacts performance, standard pressure and temperature at sea level, unstable/stable air, clouds, inversion

  • Chapter 4: everything

  • Chapter 7: military alphabet

  • Chapter 8/9: everything

  • Chapter 10: everything (the FAA wants you to be safe, so there will be a handful of questions from this section)

  • Chapter 11: NOTAMS and VFR charts (spend a good portion of your study time understanding VFR charts as many questions on the exam will be on this topic)

As of 2021, the FAA has included new questions on flight over people and remote identification. These two FAA links should provide enough information. https://www.faa.gov/uas/commercial_operators/operations_over_people/ https://www.faa.gov/uas/getting_started/remote_id/
